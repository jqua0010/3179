Livestock in Australia

In 2022, Australia’s livestock industry played a significant role in the economy, with each state contributing differently to the overall production. This visualization provides a breakdown of livestock distribution across the nation, showing how varying climates, landscapes, and agricultural practices shape the livestock landscape.
The map above illustrates the percentage share of livestock in each state, giving insight into which regions lead the country in animal agriculture. States such as Queensland and New South Wales, with their expansive grazing lands, hold the largest share of livestock. In contrast, states like Tasmania, with their smaller landmass, contribute a smaller portion but still play a critical role in specific livestock categories.
Below, we delve deeper into the different types of livestock industries across Australia. The bar chart breaks down the number of livestock by industry for the entire country, showing how beef, dairy, and sheep farming dominate the landscape. It highlights the importance of mixed farming in some regions, where producers balance sheep and cattle to diversify their output and mitigate risks. This chart helps to visualize the balance and scale of various industries within the sector, offering a clearer picture of how Australia’s livestock industry is structured.
This chart visualizes livestock trends over time, helping you understand how livestock numbers have changed across different states and categories. By exploring the filters, you can compare trends across regions, livestock types, and industry classifications. It's a valuable tool for identifying key patterns in agricultural production and shifts in industry dynamics over the years. Be sure to check out the trends across the different industry types!
